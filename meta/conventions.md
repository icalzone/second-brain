# Second Brain Conventions

This repository is designed for **fast capture** and **AI‑assisted organization**.

## Core Principles

- Capture first, organize later
- Structure emerges during synthesis, not ingestion
- Raw files are never edited or deleted
- Git history is part of the knowledge

---

## The `raw/` Folder (CRITICAL)

- `raw/` is a flat inbox
- All captures go directly into `raw/`
- There are no categories at capture time

### Rules
- Do NOT create subfolders inside `raw/`
- Do NOT decide whether something is a note, link, log, or idea
- Do NOT add metadata to raw files
- Filenames may include dates or short hints, but nothing more

✅ If you hesitate about where something belongs, it belongs in `raw/`.

---

## The `wiki/` Folder

- All structure lives here
- Pages are organized by AI and refined by humans
- YAML metadata is allowed and encouraged
- Links are preferred over duplication

---

## Human vs AI Responsibilities

### Humans
- Capture raw material
- Review and refine synthesized knowledge
- Decide what matters

### AI
- Summarize raw inputs
- Extract concepts, projects, systems, and entities
- Maintain cross‑links and indexes
- Propose structural improvements

---

## Non‑Goals

- Perfect categorization at capture time
- Manual tagging systems
- Folder structures that encode meaning

Simplicity at input produces clarity at output.
