# Credit Freeze

A credit freeze (also called a security freeze) restricts access to your credit report, making it nearly impossible for identity thieves to open new accounts in your name. It is free, legally protected, and does not affect your credit score.

## What It Does

When your credit is frozen, lenders cannot pull your credit file to approve new loans or credit cards — your report is effectively locked. The freeze stays in place permanently until you remove it.

**What a freeze protects against:** Unauthorized new accounts opened in your name.  
**What it does NOT protect against:** Fraud on existing accounts (e.g., a stolen credit card number).

## How to Freeze Your Credit (2026)

You must contact **all three major credit bureaus individually**. Freezing only one is not sufficient — lenders may pull from different bureaus.

| Bureau | Phone | Website |
|---|---|---|
| **Equifax** | 1-800-685-1111 | freeze.equifax.com |
| **Experian** | 1-888-397-3742 | experian.com/freeze |
| **TransUnion** | 1-888-909-8872 | freeze.transunion.com |

Online is the fastest method and typically takes effect immediately.

### Steps

1. **Create an account** with each bureau to manage the freeze
2. **Provide your details** — Social Security number, date of birth, and address history
3. **Store your PIN/Password securely** — you'll need it to thaw the freeze later
4. **Confirm** — make sure you receive confirmation from all three bureaus

## Thawing (Temporary Lift)

When you need to apply for credit, a job, or rent an apartment, you can temporarily unfreeze ("thaw") your credit for a specific time window. You'll need your PIN/password from each bureau.

## Key Facts

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cost** | Free (legally mandated) |
| **Credit score impact** | None — freezing or thawing does not change your score |
| **Duration** | Permanent until you remove it |
| **Legitimate access** | Still allowed for existing creditors, government agencies, and background checks you authorize |

## Credit Freeze vs. Credit Lock

| | Freeze | Lock |
|---|---|---|
| **Cost** | Free | May have a monthly fee |
| **Legal protection** | Yes — backed by federal law | No federal mandate |
| **Ease of use** | Online/phone | Usually a bureau app |
| **Recommendation** | ✓ Preferred | Less protection for more cost |

> A credit lock from the bureaus may be more convenient, but it doesn't carry the same legal protections as a freeze. The freeze is the stronger option.
