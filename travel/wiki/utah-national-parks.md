# Utah National Parks — The Mighty Five

Utah is home to five world-class national parks — collectively known as the "Mighty Five" — set amid some of the most dramatic desert scenery in the American West. The full loop can be done in 7–10 days, starting and ending near Las Vegas or flying in/out of Moab.

## The Five Parks

| Park | Signature Features |
|---|---|
| **Zion** | Steep red cliffs, The Narrows, Angel's Landing |
| **Bryce Canyon** | Hoodoo spire formations, Rim Trail, high-elevation views |
| **Capitol Reef** | Waterpocket Fold, orchards, solitude |
| **Canyonlands** | Massive canyon system, Island in the Sky, river confluence |
| **Arches** | 2,000+ natural stone arches, Delicate Arch |

## Recommended Route (Loop from Las Vegas)

**Start:** Las Vegas → drive ~3 hours to Springdale (Zion gateway)

1. **Zion National Park** — iconic canyon hikes; use the shuttle system; arrive early for parking
2. **Bryce Canyon National Park** — hoodoos and spires; higher elevation = cooler weather; bring layers
3. **Kodachrome Basin State Park** *(optional detour)* — colorful rock formations, sand pipes, less crowded
4. **Capitol Reef National Park** — along Highway 12 scenic byway
5. **Canyonlands National Park** — Island in the Sky district for panoramic views
6. **Arches National Park** — near Moab; base in Moab for both Canyonlands and Arches

**Return:** Moab → I-70 → I-15 → Las Vegas (Kolob Canyon at Zion is a great final stop before returning)

## Highway 12 — Don't Skip This

Highway 12 Scenic Byway is one of the most spectacular drives in the country, connecting Bryce Canyon to Capitol Reef through the Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument. Expect sandstone cliffs, slot canyons, and high-desert vistas. In winter/fall it can be snowy — plan accordingly.

## Timing

| Season | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Spring (Apr–May)** | Best balance of weather and crowd levels |
| **Summer** | Hot, especially canyon floors; hike early morning or evening |
| **Fall (Sep–Oct)** | Beautiful light, cooling temperatures; crowds drop |
| **Winter** | Snow is possible at higher elevations (Bryce Canyon is stunning in snow); check road conditions |

Minimum recommendation: **7 days** (one day per park). To hike more and feel relaxed: **10 days**.

## Tips
- Parks can be extremely busy; book lodging and permits well in advance
- If adding time: consider **Grand Canyon** and **Page, AZ** (Antelope Canyon + Lake Powell)
- Zion requires a shuttle — private vehicles are restricted in the main canyon
- Bryce Canyon's higher elevation makes it noticeably cooler than Zion
