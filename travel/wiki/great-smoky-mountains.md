# Great Smoky Mountains

Great Smoky Mountains National Park spans over 500,000 acres across North Carolina and Tennessee — the most visited national park in the US. Its misty mountain scenery, abundant wildlife, historic Appalachian culture, and extensive waterfall network make it worthy of multiple visits.

## Top 23 Experiences

### Scenic Drives & Viewpoints
1. **Cades Cove** — stunning valley with an 11-mile loop road for driving or biking; excellent wildlife spotting (deer, black bears). Arrive early. Loop road closed to vehicles Saturday mornings (May–Sept) for bikers.
2. **Clingmans Dome** — highest point in the park; steep half-mile trail to observation tower with panoramic views. Dress warmly (cold even in summer). Road closed in winter.
3. **Roaring Fork Motor Nature Trail** — one-way narrow trail through old-growth forest; access to waterfalls and historic sites. Closed in winter due to ice.

### Hikes & Trails
4. **Alum Cave Trail** — popular route to Mount LeConte with fascinating geological formations (Alum Cave Bluffs); check conditions and carry water
5. **Rainbow Falls Trail** — 5.4-mile round trip to an 80-foot waterfall; moderately difficult; rocks near falls are slippery
6. **Appalachian Trail** — the park straddles the AT; day hike sections are accessible throughout
7. **Chimney Tops** — steep but short hike to dramatic rocky summits

### Visitor Centers
8. **Oconaluftee Visitor Center** — best starting point; maps, trail info, road closure updates; open year-round (Cherokee, NC entrance)
9. **Sugarlands Visitor Center** — near Gatlinburg, TN entrance

### Wildlife Watching
10. **Elk viewing** — best at Cataloochee Valley (east side); early morning/evening
11. **Bear watching** — throughout the park; especially Cades Cove and Clingmans Dome area
12. **Synchronous Fireflies** *(seasonal)* — late May/early June; a rare natural light show; ticketed event, lottery required

### History & Culture
13. **Mountain Farm Museum** (at Oconaluftee) — preserved historic Appalachian farm buildings
14. **Mingus Mill** — historic wooden grist mill, still operating
15. **Cataloochee Valley** — preserved historic buildings plus elk

### Towns Near the Park
16. **Gatlinburg, TN** — main gateway town; lodging, restaurants, aerial tramway
17. **Pigeon Forge, TN** — Dollywood + family attractions
18. **Cherokee, NC** — cultural center for the Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians

## Top Waterfalls

| Waterfall | Hike Distance | Difficulty | Highlights |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Abrams Falls** | 5 mi RT | Moderate | High volume flow; deep pool; in Cades Cove |
| **Grotto Falls** | 3 mi RT | Easy | Walk *behind* the waterfall; old-growth forest |
| **Laurel Falls** | 2.6 mi RT (paved) | Easy | Family-friendly; divided upper + lower sections |
| **Rainbow Falls** | 5.4 mi RT | Moderate | 80-foot drops; catches rainbows in afternoon light |
| **Ramsey Cascades** | 8 mi RT | Strenuous | Largest waterfall in the park; old-growth forest |
| **Hen Wallow Falls** | 4.4 mi RT | Moderate | Cascades through mossy forest |
| **Mouse Creek Falls** | 4 mi RT | Easy–Mod | Scenic walk in Big Creek area |
| **Indian Creek Falls** | 1.6 mi RT | Easy | Near Deep Creek; combines well with Toms Branch Falls |
| **Toms Branch Falls** | 1.6 mi RT | Easy | Near Indian Creek; short and accessible |
| **Place of a Thousand Drips** | varies | Moderate | Multi-tiered cascade along Roaring Fork |

> Always check trail conditions before heading out. Rocks near any waterfall are slippery.

## Photography at the Smokies

The Smokies are one of the **9 most scenic national parks for photography** (see [[national-park-photography]]). Best opportunities:
- **Fall foliage** — late October in the lower elevations; early October at higher elevations
- **Early morning mist** from any overlook
- **Waterfalls** — long exposures create silky effect (use a tripod)
- **Wildlife** — Cades Cove at dawn

## Timing & Crowds

- **Best months:** April–May (wildflowers, moderate temps), September–October (fall foliage, cooler)
- **Busiest:** Summer weekends, October (fall peak)
- No entry fees — the park is free to enter
- Some areas require timed entry permits during peak season — check NPS.gov before visiting
