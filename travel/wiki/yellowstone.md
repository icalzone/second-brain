# Yellowstone — One-Day Fast-Track Itinerary

Yellowstone spans nearly 3,500 square miles and could easily fill weeks of exploration. This guide focuses on making the most of a single day, concentrated on the Southern Loop — the most sight-dense route accessible from West Yellowstone or Jackson, Wyoming.

## Park Geography

Yellowstone has two main loops:

| Loop | Highlights | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Southern (Lower) Loop** | Grand Prismatic Spring, Old Faithful, geysers, hot springs | Short visits (1 day) |
| **Northern (Upper) Loop** | Lamar Valley wildlife, Mammoth Hot Springs, Tower Fall | Wildlife, more time |

> **Key closure windows:** Facilities close from September through November and from late March to early April. Plan outside these windows.

## One-Day Southern Loop Itinerary (from West Yellowstone)

### Morning
1. **Grand Prismatic Spring** — the largest hot spring in the US; vivid rainbow colors best viewed from the overlook trail
2. **Midway Geyser Basin** — boardwalk through steaming geothermal features

### Midday
3. **Old Faithful** — America's most famous geyser; erupts approximately every 90 minutes; check the posted prediction at the Visitor Center for exact timing
4. **Old Faithful Visitor Education Center** — exhibits on geothermal activity; grab lunch here

### Afternoon
5. **West Thumb Geyser Basin** — hot springs directly on the shore of Yellowstone Lake
6. **Yellowstone Lake** — scenic stop for views across the largest high-elevation lake in North America

### Late Afternoon / Evening
7. **Hayden Valley** — one of the best wildlife watching spots in the park; bison herds, watelk, bears, coyotes
8. **Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone** — stunning canyon views from Artist or Inspiration Point; Upper and Lower Falls

## Tips
- Start as early as possible — parking fills quickly at Grand Prismatic and Old Faithful
- Check geyser eruption predictions at the Visitor Center or NPS app
- Wildlife sightings are most common in the early morning and evening
- Carry layers — weather changes quickly even in summer
- If adding a second day, cover the **Northern Loop** (Lamar Valley for wolves and bears)
