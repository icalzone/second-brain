# Texas National Parks Road Trip

Texas offers a remarkable variety of national parks and historic sites, from vast desert wilderness to Gulf Coast beaches and Spanish colonial missions. A full Texas parks road trip makes a great multi-day journey through the Lone Star State.

## Key Stops

### 1. Lyndon B. Johnson National Historical Park (Texas Hill Country)
- Explore the life of America's 36th President at the LBJ Ranch ("Texas White House")
- Visit his birthplace and childhood home (carefully rebuilt)
- Nearby: LBJ Library & Museum at the University of Texas, Austin

### 2. San Antonio Missions National Historical Park
- The only **UNESCO World Heritage Site in Texas**
- Five Spanish colonial missions, each with its own story
- **Mission San José** — the "Queen of the Missions"; largest site with the Visitor Center
- **Mission Concepción** — original Spanish colonial architecture and stunning frescos
- Options: self-guided walking trails or guided tours connecting the missions

### 3. Padre Island National Seashore
- 66-mile barrier island on the Gulf Coast
- **Sea turtle hatching** — watch baby turtles make their way to the ocean
- Exceptional **stargazing** due to minimal light pollution
- Activities: beachcombing, surf fishing, kayaking through the Laguna Madre (one of few hypersaline lagoons in the world)
- Getting there: ~3 hours from Houston via Park Road 22

### 4. Palo Alto Battlefield National Historical Park
- Site of the **first major battle of the Mexican-American War (1846)**
- Trails and exhibits explain the background and significance of the conflict
- Also includes Resaca de la Palma battlefield, near Brownsville

### 5. Big Bend National Park *(not covered in source but strongly recommended)*
- One of the most remote and spectacular parks in Texas
- Vast desert, the Rio Grande, hiking, and dark sky stargazing

### 6. Guadalupe Mountains National Park
- Highest peak in Texas (Guadalupe Peak)
- Adjacent to New Mexico; great for combining into a Southwest road trip

## Tips
- Texas is massive — plan route carefully to avoid backtracking
- Padre Island is ideal for coastal nature lovers; combine with a Corpus Christi base
- San Antonio Missions and the Hill Country sites can be combined into a compact loop from San Antonio or Austin
