# RV National Parks — Top 10 Picks

A curated list of the best U.S. national parks for RV travel, with campground recommendations, reservation tips, and key attractions for each.

## 1. Yellowstone National Park (WY/MT/ID)

The crown jewel of RV travel. Best RV campgrounds (all reservable via yellowstonenationalparklodges.com or recreation.gov):
- Bridge Bay, Canyon, Fishing Bridge, Grant Village, Madison
- **Exception:** Mammoth Campground is first-come, first-served Oct 15 – Apr 1

Plan for at least **3 days** minimum; a full week is ideal. Must-sees: Old Faithful (erupts every ~90 min), Grand Prismatic Spring, Yellowstone Lake, Grand Canyon of Yellowstone.

## 2. Grand Teton National Park (WY)

300,000+ acres of pristine wilderness. 42-mile scenic loop drive with wildlife (elk, bison, moose). RV campgrounds:
- Colter Bay RV Park, Gros Ventre Campground, Colter Bay Campground (all via recreation.gov)
- Stays of 14–30 nights possible; book up to 6 months in advance

## 3. Glacier National Park (MT)

Snow-capped mountains, turquoise lakes, diverse wildlife. 13 front-country campgrounds. Key reservable sites: Apgar, Fish Creek, Avalanche, St. Mary, Many Glacier. Cell service is spotty — book before you arrive. Going-to-the-Sun Road typically open July–September only.

## 4. Yosemite National Park (CA)

Iconic Half Dome, El Capitan, Mirror Lake. Advance reservations required (timed entry). RV-specific size restrictions in some areas — check before booking. Best RV campgrounds: Upper Pines, Lower Pines, North Pines (all via recreation.gov).

## 5. Acadia National Park (ME)

The only national park in New England. Ocean Path, Cadillac Mountain (first to see sunrise in the US), carriage roads. Blackwoods and Seawall campgrounds for RVs. Seasonal timed entry permits may apply.

## 6. Zion National Park (UT)

Cathedral-like sandstone canyons. Note: private vehicles are restricted in the main canyon — park at the Visitor Center and use the free shuttle. South Campground and Watchman Campground for RVs (recreation.gov). See [[utah-national-parks]] for full Utah itinerary.

## 7. Rocky Mountain National Park (CO)

High alpine scenery: 14,000-ft peaks, Trail Ridge Road (highest continuous paved road in the US). Timed entry permits required in summer. Moraine Park and Glacier Basin campgrounds.

## 8. Olympic National Park (WA)

Three ecosystems in one park: temperate rainforest, rugged coastline, alpine meadows. Excellent for extended RV stay. Hoh Rainforest, Hurricane Ridge, Ruby Beach.

## 9. Big Bend National Park (TX)

One of the most remote and uncrowded parks. Rio Grande border scenery, desert, and Chisos Mountains. Rio Grande Village, Chisos Basin, Cottonwood campgrounds. Dark sky destination.

## 10. Great Smoky Mountains National Park (TN/NC)

The most visited park in the US — and free to enter. Elkmont, Smokemont, Cosby campgrounds for RVs. See [[great-smoky-mountains]] for full details.

## General RV Tips
- Book campgrounds 6 months in advance for summer peak season
- Check RV size restrictions (length, slide-outs) for each park before booking
- recreation.gov is the booking platform for most federal campgrounds
- Some parks now require timed entry permits separate from campsite reservations
