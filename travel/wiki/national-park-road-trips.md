# U.S. National Park Road Trips Overview

The United States offers countless road trip routes through its national parks — from the Appalachians in the East to the volcanic Cascades in the Pacific Northwest. Road trips are the quintessential way to experience them: set your own pace, create a specific itinerary, and stop wherever you like.

## Route Highlights

### 1. Blue Ridge & Great Smoky Mountains (East)
- **Start:** Skyline Drive, Shenandoah National Park, VA
- **End:** Great Smoky Mountains National Park, NC/TN
- **Connector:** Blue Ridge Parkway (469 miles along the ridge)
- **Best season:** Fall — brilliant foliage across millions of deciduous trees
- **Wildlife:** Black-tailed deer, elk, black bears, wild turkeys
- **Highlights:** Historic mountain towns, waterfalls, bluegrass music culture

### 2. Cascade Range Volcanoes (Pacific Northwest)
- Runs 700+ miles through Washington, Oregon, and into Northern California
- 15+ active major volcanoes along the route
- Key parks: **Mount Rainier**, **Crater Lake**, **Lassen Volcanic**
- Last major eruptions: Mount St. Helens (1980), Lassen Peak (1914–1920)
