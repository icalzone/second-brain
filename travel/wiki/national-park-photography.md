# National Parks for Photography

Nine of the most scenic U.S. national parks for photography — ranked for dramatic landscapes, vibrant colors, and iconic landmarks.

## The Top 9

| # | Park | State | Signature Shots |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Yosemite** | California | Half Dome at sunset, El Capitan, Mirror Lake reflections, waterfalls |
| 2 | **Yellowstone** | Wyoming | Grand Prismatic Spring colors, Old Faithful steam plumes, Grand Canyon of Yellowstone at sunrise/sunset |
| 3 | **Grand Canyon** | Arizona | Layered canyon walls, sunrise/sunset rim light |
| 4 | **Zion** | Utah | Towering red sandstone walls, The Narrows water reflections |
| 5 | **Rocky Mountain** | Colorado | Alpine meadows, elk, 14ers above tree line |
| 6 | **Great Smoky Mountains** | TN/NC | Morning mist, fall foliage, historic mills, waterfalls |
| 7 | **Acadia** | Maine | Coastal cliffs, Bass Harbor Lighthouse, rocky shores, sunrise on Cadillac Mountain |
| 8 | **Glacier** | Montana | Turquoise lakes, wildflower meadows, mountain goats |
| 9 | **Denali** | Alaska | North America's highest peak, vast wilderness, northern lights |

## Tips for Each Park

### Yosemite
- Avoid smoky conditions in late August
- Arrive early for parking and shuttle access
- Mirror Lake: Walk directly up toward Half Dome for a unique perspective

### Yellowstone
- Grand Prismatic best photographed from the overlook trail (not just the boardwalk)
- Grand Canyon of Yellowstone — Artist Point and Inspiration Point for falls photos
- Wildlife: early morning and evening in Hayden Valley

### Great Smoky Mountains
- Early morning mist is best from Newfound Gap or any high overlook
- Waterfall long exposures: bring a tripod and a 10-stop ND filter for silky water
- Fall foliage peaks mid-October at lower elevations

### Zion
- The Narrows requires waterproof boots/gear; early morning light is stunning in the canyon slot
- Angel's Landing summit offers panoramic shots but involves chains climbing

### Acadia
- Cadillac Mountain: first place in the US to see sunrise (fall–winter)
- Bass Harbor Lighthouse: golden hour from the rocks below
