## 1️⃣ What this card’s job actually is

Sunrise / Sunset is **not forecast** and **not a metric**.  
It answers one simple question:

> *“Where are we in the day’s light cycle?”*

So the card should feel:

*   Calm
*   Informational
*   Slightly poetic, but not cute

This makes it a great contrast to data‑heavy cards.

## 3️⃣ Exact microcopy rules (lock these in)

### A) Terminology (don’t vary this)

Use **only** these terms:

*   **Sunrise**
*   **Sunset**
*   **Dawn**
*   **Dusk**

Avoid:

*   ❌ “First light”
*   ❌ “Last light”
*   ❌ “Civil twilight” (too technical)

These four words are universally understood.

***

### B) Time formatting

*   Always show **AM / PM**
*   Always use **leading zero suppression** (7:06 AM, not 07:06)
*   Match the rest of your app’s time formatting exactly

Consistency matters more here than anywhere else.

***

### C) Information density rules

**Card shows**

*   Primary event time (Sunrise or Sunset)
*   One supporting time (Dawn or Dusk)

**Card does NOT show**

*   Length of day
*   Golden hour
*   Solar noon
*   Progress indicators

Those belong in an optional sheet *if you ever add one*.

***

### D) Icon usage (subtle but important)

Use:

*   Sunrise icon with **upward sun**
*   Sunset icon with **downward sun**

Rules:

*   Same size and weight as other metric icons
*   Neutral color (no yellow/orange fills on the card)
*   No animation

Let the *concept* do the work, not color.

***

## 4️⃣ Optional micro‑enhancements (still a “quick win”)

These are **safe** enhancements you can add later without changing layout.

### A) Contextual sublabel (very subtle)

Under the main time, *occasionally* add:

*   “Already occurred” (after sunrise)
*   “Coming up next” (before sunset)

Example:

    Sunset
    6:51 PM
    Coming up next

This gives time awareness without adding clocks or progress bars.

***

### B) Alignment with Today card tone

Sunrise / Sunset copy should be the *calmest* in the app.

Compare tones:

*   Today: “Rain and snow possible”
*   Metrics: “Light breeze”
*   Sunrise: just… time

That contrast is good.

***

## 5️⃣ Do you need a Sunrise / Sunset sheet?

Honestly: **no**, not right now.

If you ever add one, it should be extremely light:

**Possible sheet content**

*   Sunrise
*   Sunset
*   Dawn
*   Dusk
*   Length of day (optional)

But unless users ask for it, the card alone is perfect.

***

## ✅ Final recommendation (what I’d ship)

*   Keep **two separate cards** (Sunrise and Sunset)
*   Keep the copy exactly as above
*   No charts, no progress arcs, no narrative text
*   Let this card be the “quiet moment” in the UI

This is exactly the kind of restraint that makes the rest of the app feel more confident.
